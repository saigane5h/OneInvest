Why this layout

Drawn from Groww (clean cards, "subscribed %" front and centre) and Zerodha Kite (information density, real prices). What we changed:

  • Countdown not just date — surfaces urgency the moment the user opens the app.
  • "Subscribed 67%" — sets the right expectation; if it's already 95%, the user shouldn't expect to win.
  • Yield + min ticket + rating visible in 3 chips — the three questions every retail investor actually asks.
  • Stocks below, not hidden — a bond banner that buries the user's portfolio feels like a billboard, not a brokerage.
  • IPOs share the same card grammar — one mental model for all primary-market issuances.

1. Problem Statement

India is in the middle of a fixed-income retail boom — RBI Retail Direct crossed 1.6M accounts and corporate-bond platforms (GoldenPi, Wint, Bondbazaar) are scaling fast. But the subscription experience is broken at the seams:

  • The 60–120 second UPI gap — between mandate and bank confirmation — is unmanaged. Users either refresh frantically or watch a silent spinner.
  • "Application could not be processed" is the universal failure message — no explanation, no recovery path, no next issuance date.
  • Refunds for unallotted applications take 3–7 days on most platforms, despite NPCI mandating 2 hours. Trust quietly erodes.
  • Allotment fairness is opaque — investors don't know if FCFS, lottery, or pro-rata applied. Suspicion fills the vacuum.
  • No platform binds equities + IPOs + bonds + suitability + AI into one mental model. Zerodha is deep in equity, Groww is broad but shallow, GoldenPi is bond-only.

3. AI Thesis — where AI is the moat, where it isn't

AI does: demand prediction (auto-scales infra before 10 AM rush), bank-health monitoring (warns slow banks pre-application), fraud-cluster detection (catches bot farms on retail quota), alternative ranking (within rule-gated set), persona-aware copy generation, post-event re-engagement timing.

AI doesn't: allotment (regulator's domain), eligibility/KYC (deterministic), interest rate/yield (treasury's call), refund processing (NPCI-bound), risk-suitability gating (hard rules, not AI judgement).

"AI is the ranking engine. Rules are the gate. The gate decides who's allowed in; AI decides what they see first."

8. Out of Scope (v1)

  • Secondary-market bond trading (post-allotment liquidity is v2).
  • International bonds & offshore IPOs.

9. Open Questions
